Climate, hydrology, and machine learning for environmental systems

I am Ali Mozhdehi Fard, a PhD student at Nazarbayev University. My work integrates climate data analysis, hydrological modeling, geospatial methods, and machine learning to study drought risk, land–atmosphere interactions, and environmental change across data-scarce regions.

I build reproducible computational workflows using CMIP6 outputs, satellite observations, NASA POWER datasets, and statistical learning methods to generate interpretable insights for climate impact assessment.

About

My academic background combines hydraulic engineering, numerical modeling, and data-driven environmental analysis. I am particularly interested in how climate variability and long-term warming reshape water systems, drought patterns, vegetation response, and atmospheric processes.

Research direction

My work focuses on climate change impact assessment using observational and modeled datasets, with particular emphasis on Central Asia and related environmental systems. I use multi-model climate outputs, statistical downscaling and bias correction, remote sensing indicators, and interpretable machine learning to evaluate present and future risk.

Current themes include drought hotspot identification, LAI dynamics, radiation–dust interactions, and geospatial climate classification workflows.

Core interests

  • Climate change impact assessment using CMIP6 and SSP scenarios
  • Hydrological and environmental modeling
  • Machine learning in climate science and earth systems
  • Drought analysis using SPI, SPEI, PCA, and risk indicators
